# Product Vision Brief — Rippled

## Purpose

Rippled is a personal AI support layer that helps people forget fewer work commitments without needing to maintain another task system.

It exists to reduce the mental overhead created by modern work communication: meetings, Slack, email, and the many small promises, follow-ups, and delivery expectations that get created in passing and then partially lost in memory, context switching, and overload.

Rippled should help a user stay on top of what they said they would do, what others are now waiting on, what still lacks ownership or timing, and what appears to have been delivered, all without forcing the user into constant triage.

---

## Vision

Rippled becomes a trusted commitment intelligence assistant for one person at a time.

Its job is not to replace judgment, manage projects, or flood the user with extracted tasks. Its job is to quietly observe work communication, detect likely commitments, preserve ambiguity where needed, and surface the right things at the right time so the user feels less mental drag and fewer “oh shit, I forgot” moments.

The end state is a user who experiences more follow-through, less mental clutter, and less need to manually reconstruct what was promised, by whom, and when.

---

## The Problem Rippled Solves

In modern work, commitments are rarely created only in formal task systems.

They appear in:

- meetings  
- Slack messages and threads  
- email threads  
- quick replies  
- delegated follow-ups  
- informal “I’ll handle it” statements  
- client-facing promises  
- internal handoffs  
- unspoken next steps that become socially real even if they were never formally assigned  

These commitments vary in size. Some are large and consequential. Many are small. But small commitments create disproportionate cognitive load because they are easy to forget, difficult to track manually, and often socially costly when missed.

The burden is not only “delivering big things.” It is also:

- remembering what was promised  
- knowing whether someone is waiting  
- knowing whether ownership is clear  
- remembering when something should happen  
- realizing that a commitment was likely completed or needs follow-up  
- carrying many half-open loops in working memory  

Traditional task systems do not solve this well because they depend on deliberate entry and maintenance. Most communication systems do not solve it because they are streams, not commitment-aware systems.

Rippled exists in the gap between communication and follow-through.

---

## Core User Outcome

The core outcome Rippled should create is:

**The user feels like they forgot fewer things and remembered to follow through without having to think so hard about it.**

This does not mean perfect capture. It means meaningful reduction in cognitive burden.

A successful Rippled experience should feel like:

- fewer dropped promises  
- fewer moments of reconstructing past conversations  
- fewer unclear handoffs  
- fewer missed client-facing commitments  
- less stress from small unresolved obligations  
- more confidence that important and easy-to-miss commitments are being quietly watched  

---

## Primary User

Rippled is initially built for an individual user, not a team workspace.

The first and best-fit user is someone whose work creates many commitments across communication channels and who feels the strain of holding too much in their head.

This includes:

- founders working with contractors or small teams  
- executives with high communication volume  
- service business operators  
- account managers or sales reps with follow-through obligations  
- people whose work depends on timely replies, handoffs, deliverables, and relationship management  

The initial product model is not “many users coordinating together.” It is “one user with an intelligent assistant helping them maintain follow-through.”

---

## What Rippled Is

Rippled is:

- a communication-aware commitment intelligence system  
- a personal AI assistant for follow-through support  
- a system that observes meetings, Slack, and email  
- a layer that detects likely commitments, ambiguity, progress, and delivery signals  
- a suggestion engine that helps the user remember, review, and act  
- a trust-sensitive product that preserves uncertainty instead of pretending certainty  

---

## What Rippled Is Not

Rippled is not:

- a general meeting notes product  
- a traditional task manager  
- a project management system  
- a CRM replacement  
- a productivity dashboard for its own sake  
- an automation engine that turns every action phrase into a task  
- a system that presents AI guesses as hard truth  

Rippled should not create a new heavy maintenance burden. It should not require the user to continually clean up AI output. It should not become another inbox of obligations.

---

## Product Thesis

The product thesis behind Rippled is:

**A large share of cognitive load at work comes not only from major deadlines, but from the accumulation of small and medium commitments made across communication channels. If those commitments can be quietly captured, clarified, and surfaced with restraint, users can feel meaningfully less mental strain without adopting another full task system.**

This thesis implies several important beliefs:

1. Small commitments matter more than most systems account for.  
2. Communication is where commitments are born, updated, and completed.  
3. Users need help reconstructing obligations, not just storing tasks.  
4. Trust is more important than aggressiveness.  
5. Suggestion quality matters more than extraction volume.  
6. The system should capture more than it surfaces.  
7. The system should infer carefully and speak tentatively.  

---

## Primary Sources of Truth

Rippled should treat these as first-class sources of commitment signals:

- meetings  
- Slack  
- email  

Each of these channels can serve three roles:

- source of new commitments  
- source of clarification  
- source of progress or completion evidence  

This matters because real work does not happen in one place. A commitment may begin in a meeting, get clarified in Slack, and be delivered via email. Rippled should unify that story instead of treating each channel as isolated.

Over time, additional sources may be added, but the product vision should remain centered on communication-derived commitments rather than formal task entry.

---

## The Kind of Commitments Rippled Should Care About

Rippled should care about both:

- **big promises**
- **small commitments**

### Big promises

These are more likely to be:

- external or client-facing  
- tied to explicit timing  
- tied to clear deliverables  
- higher consequence if missed  

These belong in the main commitments view.

### Small commitments

These are more likely to be:

- internal  
- informal  
- quick follow-ups  
- “I’ll look into it”  
- “I’ll send that over”  
- “Let me check”  
- small delivery or response obligations  

These still matter because they create cognitive load and social expectation. They belong in a lighter shortlist, not necessarily in the same place as major commitments.

Rippled should recognize that importance and mental burden are related but not identical.

---

## Product Experience Goal

Rippled should feel like a thoughtful assistant, not a surveillance engine and not a nagging workflow tool.

The desired feeling is:

- “good catch”  
- “right, I did say that”  
- “glad this surfaced now”  
- “helpful that this was connected”  
- “nice, it looks like this was probably delivered”  
- “this makes my work feel lighter”  

The undesired feeling is:

- “why is this showing me everything?”  
- “this is just another task manager”  
- “this keeps asking me to clarify trivial things”  
- “this sounds more certain than it should”  
- “now I have one more system to maintain”  

---

## Core Product Principles

### 1. Reduce cognitive load, not just missed deadlines

The goal is not only to catch major work items. It is to reduce the mental burden of many unresolved obligations, especially the small ones.

### 2. Capture broadly, surface selectively

Rippled should retain more signals internally than it presents to the user. Surfaced output must be tighter than captured output.

### 3. Suggest, do not declare

The system should avoid presenting interpretations as absolute truth. It should use language that reflects confidence and preserves uncertainty.

### 4. Preserve ambiguity instead of fabricating certainty

If ownership, timing, or completion is unclear, Rippled should represent that honestly rather than resolve it too aggressively.

### 5. Communication context matters

A commitment should be understood across linked signals, not only in the single message or meeting where it first appeared.

### 6. External promises deserve stricter treatment

Client-facing and externally visible commitments generally carry heavier consequences and should be treated with greater strictness and urgency.

### 7. Silent observation before interruption

The system should often wait for additional signals before prompting for clarification, especially when later communication may naturally resolve ambiguity.

### 8. Trust beats cleverness

A smaller number of high-value suggestions is better than a larger number of impressive but noisy inferences.

---

## Trust Model

Rippled should be assertive in internal detection, but restrained in user-facing communication.

That means:

- it is acceptable to capture more candidate signals internally  
- it is not acceptable to surface weak interpretations as if they are reliable  
- explicit commitments should be prioritized for capture  
- missing a real explicit commitment is worse than storing some false internal candidates  
- surfaced interruptions should remain sparse and useful  
- the product should always sound like it is helping the user think, not replacing the user’s judgment  

Rippled should never rely on a tone of certainty it has not earned.

---

## Commitment Lifecycle Vision

Rippled should not think only in terms of “open” and “done.”

The product should eventually support at least a distinction between:

- active  
- delivered  
- closed  

This matters because something can be delivered but not yet truly closed, and it can later reopen based on feedback, revision, or additional requests.

Rippled should help the user track the real state of obligations, not force an overly simplistic task model onto communication-shaped work.

---

## Surface Vision

Rippled should not present everything in one undifferentiated list.

The initial surface model should likely include:

- **Main**: bigger, more consequential commitments  
- **Shortlist**: smaller commitments that still matter cognitively  
- **Clarifications**: items that may need user review when ambiguity remains unresolved  

These surfaces should help the user quickly regain orientation, not spend time administering the system.

Pushes, digests, and messages should be even more selective than the in-app surfaces.

---

## Success Criteria for the Product Vision

Rippled is succeeding when users experience:

- fewer forgotten commitments  
- fewer dropped client-facing promises  
- fewer unresolved ownership gaps  
- less need to manually reconstruct conversations  
- useful reminders and suggestions that feel well-timed  
- lower mental drag from small unresolved obligations  
- growing trust that the system is quietly watching what matters  

Rippled is not succeeding if users feel:

- overwhelmed by surfaced items  
- burdened by correction and cleanup  
- skeptical of what the system claims  
- pressured to maintain another operational layer  
- interrupted too often for low-value reasons  

---

## Non-Negotiable Vision Constraints

The product should not drift into:

- generic note-taking  
- project management bloat  
- noisy AI extraction  
- high-interruption behavior  
- certainty theater  
- over-reliance on manual maintenance  

Every major product and technical decision should be checked against this question:

**Does this reduce the user’s mental burden around commitments, or does it create a new form of burden?**

If it creates new burden, it is probably wrong for Rippled.

---

## MVP Vision Implication

For MVP, Rippled does not need to solve everything.

It does need to prove the central promise:

- that communication-derived commitments can be detected usefully  
- that ambiguity can be preserved without making the system feel dumb  
- that small commitments can be surfaced in a helpful way  
- that meetings, Slack, and email can be unified into one follow-through story  
- that the user can feel lighter, not busier, because Rippled is present  

That is the real product bar.

---

## One-Sentence Vision

**Rippled helps a person forget fewer work commitments by quietly turning communication into trustworthy follow-through support, without becoming another task system.**
